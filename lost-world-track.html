Some regions of the Amazon remain so remote that they see fewer human visitors than the slopes of Everest. Our Lost World expeditions venture into these pristine territories—not as conquests but as privileged journeys into Earth's last great wilderness. These challenging multi-day treks reward the adventurous with experiences increasingly rare in our connected world: genuine exploration.

The Experience

Your expedition begins with thorough preparation at our base camp, where guides assess equipment and brief you on the journey ahead. Then you'll depart civilization, trekking through territories where satellite phones provide the only connection to the outside world. Each day brings new challenges and discoveries—from river crossings to ascents of isolated tepuis (table mountains) that harbor endemic species.

Camping in specially designed hammock systems that leave no trace, you'll experience the rainforest's full sensory immersion—from the dawn chorus that begins in total darkness to the symphony of insects that accompanies nightfall. Our expedition leaders, combining wilderness medicine training with deep ecological knowledge, ensure both your safety and understanding throughout the journey.

Key Features

  • True Remoteness: Our routes access regions visited by fewer than 100 people annually
  • Scientific Purpose: Each expedition contributes to ongoing biodiversity surveys and mapping projects
  • Appropriate Challenge: Expeditions graded by difficulty allow you to select the adventure matching your experience
  • Ethical Exploration: All routes developed with indigenous territorial knowledge and permission
